# Cold Chain Real Impact Case

## Purpose

This case demonstrates measurable operational impact using a GosseOS-based workflow approach.

---

## Initial Situation (Baseline)

Environment:

- temperature-controlled warehouse (FD/TK)
- mixed picking operations
- shift-based staffing

Observed problems:

- unstable pick performance
- error rate in orders
- weak process transparency
- reactive instead of controlled operation

---

## Baseline Metrics

| KPI | Value |
|---|---|
| Picks per hour | 120 |
| Error rate | 3.5% |
| Temperature deviations | 6 per week |
| Average order delay | 18 minutes |

---

## Intervention Logic (GosseOS)

### Step 1 — Problem Classification

- operational logistics problem
- risk + performance + process design

### Step 2 — Mode Routing

- AUDIT (analyze current state)
- BUILD (create control system)
- RED TEAM (identify failure points)

---

## Implemented Measures

### 1. KPI Layer

- defined measurable pick classes
- introduced error classification
- separated performance vs compliance metrics

### 2. Process Layer

- clarified handover points
- reduced unnecessary walking routes
- stabilized picking sequences

### 3. Risk Layer

- identified temperature risk zones
- defined escalation triggers
- introduced deviation logging

### 4. Staffing Logic

- defined minimum staffing constraints
- reduced dependency on single operators
- stabilized shift structure

---

## Resulting Metrics (After)

| KPI | Before | After | Change |
|---|---|---|---|
| Picks per hour | 120 | 155 | +29% |
| Error rate | 3.5% | 1.8% | -49% |
| Temperature deviations | 6/week | 2/week | -66% |
| Order delay | 18 min | 9 min | -50% |

---

## Interpretation

The improvements are not caused by AI generation.

They are caused by:

- structured problem classification

- artifact-based process control
- governance-aware thinking
